This is actually my second Chinese hand-wound watch. I previously owned a Garton pilot watch (probably using the exact same Unitas 6497 clone movement) that I also really loved. But one day, I made the poor decision of letting it get near-soaking wet. The dial casement popped off not long after, and then the movement just plain stopped working. I could have gotten it repaired, but it would have costed better than 50% of the original sub-$100 cost.

I was drawn to pilot watches because of the legibility of the dial, which traditionally uses heavy white sans-serif numerals on black dials, with generous luminous paint applied. And I was drawn to hand-wound movements because I wouldn’t be wearing it regularly, and I appreciated the simplicity of being able to pick it up, set the time, and wind it whenever I was in the mood to wear it.

Months after my Garton broke, I finally ordered this 44mm Parnis pilot watch from Manbushijie.com, which according to forums like Watchuseek

It turns out I like winding big-crown watches. It’s remotely therapeutic to me, like tapping my fingers when I’m stuck in thought. So I rarely let the power spring run out just from habitual winding.
