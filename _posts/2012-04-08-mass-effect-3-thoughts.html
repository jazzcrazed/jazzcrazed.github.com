Being a big fan of the first two games, I anticipated Mass Effect 3 about as much as the whole of the gaming world did. I just beat it a couple of weeks ago, and it left a somewhat unsatisfying taste in my mouth.

For those who are new to the series, Mass Effect is a trilogy of action RPGs following the exploits of Commander Shepard as he attempts to save sentient life across the galaxy from an ancient robotic race called the Reapers. It’s a space opera with immense production values, setting a high bar with graphics, acting, and cinematic atmosphere — all the more impressive being a game with a branching storyline and a variety of possible outcomes. But the third game never quite escapes the confines of its mechanic to tell its story compellingly, making the melodrama fall flat.

Most of the problem, I feel, lies in its attempts to flesh out a realistic world and galaxy, and not quite hitting the mark.
