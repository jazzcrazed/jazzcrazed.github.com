--- layout: post title: "Minefold for Casual Minecrafting" tags: [gaming] date: 2012-05-28 21:42:00 -0400 ---

Like millions of others in the world, I’m a big fan of the block-moving timesink, Minecraft. Not long after my curiosity was piqued by a random video of someone burning down their pixellated home, I bought a license while the game was still in Alpha, and have moved countless blocks since.

But what’s given it such lasting appeal to me wasn’t just the unique spontaneity of its endless worlds, or the dumbfounding creativity spawned of its emergent gameplay. It was the fact that its vast worlds persisted, and that, being the setter of my own rules, I could consume it at my leisure. It was something I could add to when I felt like it.
